Time Periods in World History

Common Era (CE) is one of the year notations used for the Gregorian calendar (and its predecessor, the Julian calendar), the world's most widely used calendar era. Before the Common Era (BCE) is the era before CE. BCE and CE are alternatives to the Dionysian BC and AD notations, respectively.

  • Antiquity (4,500 BCE - 476 CE)
    The ancient past
    From the beginnings of Western civilization to the beginning of the Middle Ages.
    The two principal civilizations of early Antiquity are those of Mesopotamia[1] and Egypt.
    Philosophy in this period is termed as ancient philosophy.

  • Middle ages (476 CE - 1350 CE)
    The phrase “Middle Ages” to describe Europe between the fall of Rome in 476 CE and the beginning of the Renaissance in the 14th century.
    Philosophy in this period is termed as medieval philosophy.

  • Renaissance (1350 CE - 1620 CE)
    It began in Italy about 1350 and in the rest of Europe after 1450 and that it lasted until about 1620.
    It was a fervent[2] period of European cultural, artistic, political and economic “rebirth” following the Middle Ages.
